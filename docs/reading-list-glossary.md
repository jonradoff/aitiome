## Glossary

### Toxicology & AOPs
- **AOP (Adverse Outcome Pathway):** a structured causal chain from an initiating molecular event to an adverse health outcome, used to reason about chemical hazard mechanistically.
- **MIE (Molecular Initiating Event):** the first step — a chemical interacting with a biomolecule (e.g., inhibiting complex I).
- **KE / KER (Key Event / Key Event Relationship):** the intermediate biological steps, and the evidence-weighted causal links between them.
- **AO (Adverse Outcome):** the endpoint of regulatory relevance (e.g., parkinsonian motor deficits).
- **NAM (New Approach Methodology):** any non-animal method — in silico, in chemico, in vitro — for hazard/risk assessment.
- **QSAR:** quantitative structure–activity relationship — predicting activity/toxicity from chemical structure (your Church-era work; now deep-learning-powered).
- **Read-across:** inferring a data-poor chemical's properties from structurally/mechanistically similar chemicals.
- **ToxCast / Tox21 / invitrodb:** EPA/federal high-throughput screening programs and the assay database; thousands of chemicals × hundreds of assays.
- **HTTr / HTPP:** high-throughput transcriptomics / phenotypic profiling (Cell Painting) on chemicals.
- **DNT-IVB:** developmental neurotoxicity in-vitro battery (neurite outgrowth, network formation, etc.).
- **DTXSID / DSSTox:** EPA's chemical identifier system — the correct key for resolving a chemical (and its salt form).

### Neurodegeneration
- **Substantia nigra / dopaminergic neurons:** the midbrain region and cell type whose loss defines Parkinson's motor symptoms.
- **α-synuclein / Lewy body:** the aggregation-prone protein and the intracellular inclusion that are PD pathological hallmarks.
- **Amyloid-β / tau / neurofibrillary tangles:** the two protein hallmarks of Alzheimer's (plaques and tangles).
- **APOE4:** the major common genetic risk allele for late-onset Alzheimer's.
- **Braak staging:** a scheme for how pathology progresses/ascends through the brain over disease course.
- **Neuroinflammation / microglia:** brain immune activation; central in AD, increasingly studied in PD.
- **Complex I / oxidative stress / proteostasis:** mitochondrial respiratory complex, reactive-oxygen damage, and protein-quality-control — shared neurodegeneration mechanisms and your AOP-3 core.
- **Blood-brain barrier (BBB) / neurovascular unit:** the barrier controlling what reaches the brain (your plausibility gate).
- **MPTP:** a contaminant that caused sudden parkinsonism in humans — the founding proof an environmental chemical can cause PD.

### Methods & omics
- **scRNA-seq / snRNA-seq:** single-cell / single-nucleus RNA sequencing — expression per individual cell/nucleus.
- **Spatial transcriptomics:** gene expression mapped to physical location in tissue.
- **UMAP / t-SNE:** dimensionality-reduction methods for visualizing cell clusters.
- **Cell atlas / marker genes / annotation:** cataloging cell types by their characteristic gene expression.
- **GWAS / polygenic risk score / eQTL:** associating variants with traits; aggregating variant risk; variants that affect gene expression.
- **CRISPR screen / Perturb-seq:** systematically perturbing genes; Perturb-seq adds a single-cell RNA readout per perturbation.
- **MPRA:** massively parallel reporter assay — testing thousands of regulatory sequences at once.

### AI for biology
- **Foundation model:** a large model pretrained on massive unlabeled data, then adapted to many tasks.
- **Protein / genomic language model (ESM, Evo):** transformers trained on protein or DNA sequences; predict structure, function, or variant effects.
- **GNN (graph neural network):** a network operating on graph-structured data (e.g., molecules as atom-bond graphs).
- **AlphaFold / docking / binding affinity:** AI protein-structure prediction; predicting how/whether a small molecule binds a target (your Boltz-2 lead).

### Data resources
- **CTD:** curated chemical–gene–disease associations (`DirectEvidence` = curated; inferred = noise).
- **AOP-Wiki / AOP-KB:** the AOP databases.
- **NeurotoxKb:** a dedicated neurotoxicity knowledge base.
- **MitoCarta:** the curated inventory of mitochondrial genes/proteins.
- **FAERS:** FDA Adverse Event Reporting System — real-world drug adverse-event data (disproportionality analysis).
- **GWAS Catalog / Open Targets / PubChem / UniProt / Reactome:** the broader biomedical database ecosystem.
