## ★ Revision flag (recon / plan assumption changed)

My plan §1 asserted: *"no WPHA/WNT‑endorsed AOP terminates in an Alzheimer's‑specific outcome … AD can't
be a mirror of PD."* That is **too strong and partly wrong.** Corrected picture:

- **AOP‑12 is WPHA/WNT‑ENDORSED** — "Chronic binding of antagonist to NMDARs during brain development
  leads to **neurodegeneration with impairment in learning and memory in aging**." Its endorsed narrative
  explicitly discusses **amyloid plaque deposition and tau hyperphosphorylation**, and its registered
  stressor is **lead (Pb)** — an environmental chemical. (Confirmed in our own `aop_neuro.csv`:
  `12,…,WPHA/WNT Endorsed,True`.) [aopwiki.org/aops/12](https://aopwiki.org/aops/12)
- **AOP‑48 is WPHA/WNT‑ENDORSED** — adult‑brain glutamate excitotoxicity → neuronal cell death →
  learning/memory impairment; registered stressors **domoic acid, glufosinate** (OECD Series No. 6).
  [aopwiki.org/aops/48](https://aopwiki.org/aops/48)
- **KE‑188 (neuroinflammation) is the *identical* node** shared by the endorsed PD AOP‑3 (188↔890 loop)
  and the AD AOP‑429 — a genuine, citable cross‑disease bridge.

So AD is **not** confined to two non‑endorsed AOPs. It can be anchored on an *endorsed* aging‑
neurodegeneration/memory spine (AOP‑12/48) with a disease‑specific tau/amyloid overlay (429/475), wired
to PD through KE‑188 — much closer to PD‑parity than the plan assumed. **Nuance to stay honest about:**
no AOP has amyloid/tau as its *terminal* AO; AOP‑12's MIE is a *developmental* NMDAR event that manifests
as aging neurodegeneration. AD‑specificity still comes from the 429/475 overlay + CTD‑AD DirectEvidence,
which are non‑endorsed / curated. The endorsement tiering stays — but AD's *floor* is now "endorsed
aging‑neurodegeneration AOP," not "no endorsed AOP."

## 3. Gene‑level grounding for the AD adverse outcome (facet 3)

Usable, non‑circular grounding (display + confidence weighting only — never a recovery gate):

- **AD‑microglia (GWAS, Bellenguez 2022 Nat Genet, 75 loci):** `TREM2, INPP5D, SPI1, MS4A4A, MS4A6A,
  PLCG2, ABI3, CD33, ABCA7, SORL1, BIN1, CR1, APOE, CLU, PTK2B, SPPL2A, ADAM17` (most microglia‑restricted:
  TREM2/INPP5D/SPI1/MS4A/PLCG2/ABI3/SPPL2A).
- **Disease‑Associated Microglia (DAM, Keren‑Shaul 2018 Cell):** UP `TREM2, TYROBP, APOE, CST7, LPL, ITGAX,
  CD9, SPP1, CLEC7A, CTSB/D/L, GPNMB, AXL, CD63, LGALS3`; homeostatic (lost) `P2RY12, P2RY13, CX3CR1,
  TMEM119, HEXB`.
- **Human microglial states (Sun/Mathys 2023 Cell, ROSMAP, 194k nuclei):** MG0 homeostatic (`P2RY12,
  CX3CR1`) → inflammatory MG2/MG8/MG10 (`IL1B, CCL3, RELB, LRRK2`). Human ≠ mouse DAM (Mathys 2019: only
  5/22 overlap) — cite for honesty.
- **Amyloid vs tau microglia (Gerrits 2021 Acta Neuropathol):** AD1 (amyloid, DAM‑like: `LPL, ITGAX, SPP1`)
  vs AD2 (tau/homeostatic).
- **Amyloid/tau/APP spine (canonical):** `APP, PSEN1, PSEN2, BACE1, ADAM10, NCSTN, APH1A/B, PSENEN` |
  `MAPT, GSK3B, CDK5, DYRK1A, MARK4` | neuroinflammation `NLRP3, CASP1, PYCARD, TNF, IL1B, IL6, C1QA/B/C,
  C3, CR1`. Clean mechanistic edge: **INPP5D (GWAS) → NLRP3 → IL1B** neuroinflammation.
- **AD portals (Portal, Agora, TREAT‑AD, AMP‑AD, HuMicA):** gene/omics only — **no chemical→AD layer.**
  Good for grounding/target‑confidence, NOT a chemical recovery signal. Chemical→AD remains CTD (+AlzKB,
  circular). Same anti‑diagnostic discipline as PD applies to any omic‑perturbation similarity.

## 4. AOP‑based env→AD frameworks (facet 4)

- **The AD‑AOP effort = Tsamou, Pistollato, Roggen** (JAD 2021, 2022; JAD‑Reports 2022): the tau‑driven
  AOP‑429 blueprint + a **catalog of 60+ environmental MIE "plug‑ins"** mapped onto it — pesticides
  (chlorpyrifos/malathion via AChE; dieldrin/lindane via GABA‑R; deltamethrin via GSK3β/PP2A), metals
  (lead→NMDAR/BDNF; cadmium, methylmercury, manganese→oxidative stress/tau). **Hypotheses, not endorsed
  KERs** — but a ready‑made, citable MIE list that aligns with our facet‑2 positives.
- **Bal‑Price & Meek 2017** (*Pharmacol Ther*): neurotoxicity AOPs share KEs (neuroinflammation, mito
  dysfunction, oxidative stress) and assemble into **networks**; AD flagged as a *future* application.
- **ONTOX** (EU, 2021–26): AOP network for **developmental** decreased cognition (oxidative stress + ↓T4
  hubs) — developmental, not adult AD, but methodologically relevant.
- **Bottom line:** citable support to expand exists, but everything AD‑specific (429, 475, nanoparticle,
  MIE plug‑ins) is **proposed/under‑development**; the only *endorsed* adult memory‑loss AOPs are the
  excitotoxicity ones (48 / OECD No.6) and AOP‑12; the only formally shared PD↔AD node is **KE‑188**.

## 5. Adversarial AD decoys (facet 5) — a sharper falsification than PD

The compounds most active on AD‑relevant assays are **treatments and health‑food polyphenols** — a
beautiful specificity trap:

- **Polyphenol aggregation‑inhibitor decoys (PAINS/frequent‑hitters):** curcumin, EGCG, resveratrol,
  myricetin, rosmarinic acid — hit Aβ/tau ThT, BACE1, AChE readouts; dietary; zero curated AD causation.
- **AD‑drug decoys (maximal AD‑target activity, but they *treat* AD):** donepezil, galantamine, tacrine
  (AChE), methylene blue (tau‑aggregation inhibitor), lithium (GSK3β inhibitor).
- **Parallel‑to‑PD reuse:** simvastatin, diethyl phthalate, D‑glucose, sucrose, sodium chloride.
- **Rejected as decoys (too plausibly causal — do NOT use):** chlorpyrifos (AChE + AD epi; it's a PD
  positive already), bisphenol A (emerging AD literature).

The narrative: *"the strongest signals on every AD assay are the anti‑amyloid drugs and turmeric — an
activity model would flag the cure. Aitiome doesn't, because it reasons on curated causation."*

## 6. What this lets us expand (verdict → feeds plan §2, §4)

1. **Endorsement‑anchored AD spine:** AOP‑12 (endorsed; neurodegeneration+memory in aging; **lead**) +
   AOP‑48 (endorsed; adult excitotoxicity→memory) as the endorsed floor — **replaces "no endorsed AOP."**

4. **Positives:** ~8–9 defensible discrete chemicals (DDE, lead, cadmium, DDT, arsenic, chlorpyrifos/OP,

6. **Decoys:** the polyphenol + AD‑drug trap (sharper than PD's mito decoys).
