## 3. The caveat to pre-empt — Nakamura PNAS 2008 (complex‑I "not required")
**Choi WS, …, Nakamura K. "Phenotypic differences in the induction of DA‑neuron death by rotenone, MPP+, and
paraquat" / complex‑I not required.** *PNAS* 2008; doi:10.1073/pnas.0807581105. Finding: rotenone kills DA neurons
below full complex‑I inhibition; paraquat is a very weak complex‑I inhibitor (acts via cytosolic redox cycling).

**Our honest answer (add to Anticipated Critiques, in‑app):** the OECD **AOP‑3** MIE (complex‑I binding) is the
*endorsed causal scaffold anchor*, not a claim that complex‑I inhibition is the sole necessary mechanism. Aitiome
(a) treats assay/bioactivity as **anti‑diagnostic** and never gates on it — so it does not assert "complex‑I
activity ⇒ PD"; (b) grades on **curated** causation, not the MIE mechanism; and (c) already models **convergent
routes** — paraquat is recovered via the **redox‑cycling AOP‑593**, and the complex II/III/IV family (587/588/589)
converges on the same KEs. So Nakamura 2008 is *consistent with*, not a refutation of, our framing — it's exactly
why we anchor on curated evidence + a convergent AOP family rather than a single activity assay. **Cite it as
support for the anti‑diagnostic stance.**
